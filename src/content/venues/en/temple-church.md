Built by the Knights Templar in 1185, Temple Church is one of London's most architecturally striking buildings. The round nave — modelled on the Church of the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusalem — contains stone effigies of medieval knights that have lain undisturbed for eight centuries. The transition from the circular Romanesque west end to the elongated Gothic chancel creates a dramatic visual contrast that photographers find irresistible. Featured in The Da Vinci Code, the church combines historical gravitas with a quality of light that shifts throughout the day. Early morning and late afternoon produce the most compelling results.
