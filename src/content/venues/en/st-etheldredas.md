St Etheldreda's is a survivor. Built around 1290 during the reign of Edward I, it is one of only two remaining buildings in London from that period. The chapel is intimate — seating fewer than a hundred — and its spectacular stained glass windows fill the narrow space with coloured light on sunny days. Below the chapel, the crypt dates from 1251 and offers a completely different aesthetic: low stone ceilings, candlelight, and a sense of deep antiquity. The contrast between the luminous upper chapel and the shadowed crypt gives photographers two distinct visual worlds within a single venue.
