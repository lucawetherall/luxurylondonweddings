Bath Abbey's fan-vaulted ceiling is one of the finest achievements of English Perpendicular Gothic architecture. Light pours through fifty-two windows — the abbey has more window than wall — creating an interior brightness unusual in medieval churches. Its position in the heart of Bath means a single day can combine the abbey interior with the Royal Crescent, Pulteney Bridge, and the Roman Baths. The combination of ecclesiastical grandeur and Georgian cityscape within walking distance makes Bath the most efficient multi-location shooting day outside London. The warm honey stone of the city photographs consistently well in all seasons.
