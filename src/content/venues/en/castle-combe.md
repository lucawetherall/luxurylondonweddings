Castle Combe has been called the prettiest village in England, and it is difficult to argue. Honey-coloured Cotswold stone cottages line a single street that descends to a medieval packhorse bridge over the By Brook. St Andrew's Church, with its 13th-century tower, sits above the village and offers both an intimate interior and sweeping views across the valley. The medieval market cross provides a natural centrepiece for outdoor compositions. The entire village is the set — no distracting modern buildings, no overhead wires, no anachronisms. For couples who want the quintessential English countryside, Castle Combe delivers it without compromise.
