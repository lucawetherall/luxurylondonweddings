Founded in 1123, St Bartholomew the Great is one of London's oldest surviving churches. Its heavy Norman columns and brooding medieval atmosphere have made it a favoured film location — Shakespeare in Love, Four Weddings and a Funeral, and The Other Boleyn Girl were all shot here. The ambulatory and Lady Chapel provide intimate, photogenic spaces with filtered light that rewards careful composition. Morning shoots benefit from the east window, which throws warm shafts of light across the stone floor. This is not a bright, cheerful church. It is dramatic, weighty, and profoundly atmospheric.
